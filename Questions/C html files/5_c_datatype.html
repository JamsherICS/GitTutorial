• int: Used for whole numbers in a typical range of -2,147,483,648 to 2,147,483,647.
  • float: Used for decimal numbers with single precision, typically providing 6-7 decimal digits of precision.
  • char: Used for single characters, typically representing ASCII characters with values from -128 to 127 or 0 to 255.
  • double: Used for decimal numbers with double precision, typically providing 15-16 decimal digits of precision.
  • void: Represents no value or a generic pointer.
  • _Bool: Used for true/false values, typically occupying 1 byte of memory.
  • short: Used for smaller whole numbers in a typical range of -32,768 to 32,767.
  • long: Used for larger whole numbers in a typical range of -2,147,483,648 to 2,147,483,647 (same as int on most systems).
  • long long: Used for very large whole numbers in a typical range of -9,223,372,036,854,775,808 to 9,223,372,036,854,775,807.
  • unsigned: Used for non-negative whole numbers, typically doubling the positive range of its corresponding signed type.
  • signed: Used for whole numbers that can be positive or negative. It's often implicit and not explicitly stated.
